America's Best Coffee & Donuts

Creative Brief

Project Background

  • Client: America's Best Coffee & Donut
  • Product/Service: Coffee & Donuts
  • SWOT: SWOT Anaylsis
  • Existing Research: No existing research available at this time.
  • Date: May 2021

Overview

  • Project: A website redesign for America's Best Coffee & Donuts, a local coffee shop located in Mansfield, Texas.
  • Reason for Project: Design a mobile-first website that successfully increases customer engagement for America's Best Coffee & Donuts.
  • Opportunities: Their website has a lot of room to grow and improve on rather than start from nothing. They have enough information to get a sense of the company while also having the oppourtunity to expand their brand identity.

Drivers

  • Goals: Strengthen the brand image, popularity and customer loyalty of America's Best Coffee & Donuts.
  • Top three objectives:
    • Strengthen their brand image which market survey shows an increase in brand loyalty by 15%.
    • Increase customer engagement through website and social media traffic which increases by 25%.
    • Increase revenue by 15% through social media marketing and promotion.
  • Project length: 4 months
  • Length of time until goals are met: One year after publishing website.

Audience

  • Target Audience:
    • Age: 3-30
    • Sex: Both male and female
    • Education: High School Diploma or greater
    • Income Level: Middle Class Income
    • Groups: Coffee connoisseurs, students, couples, families, and people with a caffeine addiction.
  • What do they think of us: America's Best Coffee & Donuts is a great place to meet people, hang out and relax. On top of the great coffee and donuts, we have a creative, fun and different vibe than what can be found at any random Starbucks.
  • Why they should care about us: We're local, have great products, friendly service, cycling menu options, and a drive thru. We also hire high school students bringing in the local community for a family feel when coming to our coffee shop.

Competitors

  • Direct competition:
    • Coffee franchises
    • Small local coffee shops
  • What we should take away from them:
    • Talk about coffee suppliers and coffee bean origins/story.
    • Promotion of new drinks, special deals, and introduce loyalty program.
  • What makes us unique from the competition: We sell coffee and donuts that are made fresh daily by our employees. We sell a variety of drinks like boba and provide really friend;y and fast service.

Tone

  • What tone should we use to communicate to our target audience and why: A friendly, upbeat and energetic enviornment to showcase the energy boost our coffee. We're knowledgeable about the coffee we serve without coming off as pretentious but personable.
  • What adjectives describe the feeling we are trying to protect or our approach:
    • Friendly
    • Knowledgeable
    • Welcoming
    • Warm
    • Homey

Message

  • What are we saying with this piece: We're a fantastic local coffee shop that provides excellent coffee and donuts quickly and with a smile.
  • Are the words already development or do we need to develop them: We can defintely develop the words a little bit more.
  • We want the audience(s) to view our website and think: We're a warm, inviting local coffee shop with an excellent selection of coffee, boba and donuts.

Visuals

  • Are we developing new images or picking up existing ones: We'll pick up existing images from Unsplash.com and from America's Best Coffee and Donuts. Providing photo credit and permissions for all visuals used.

Details

  • Mandatory information that must be included: The menu page, location, contact information, logo and about us page.
  • Deliverables: Updated brand design, logo and mobile-friendly website.
  • Format parameters: HTML/CSS (Flexbox, Grid)
  • Limitations/restrictions; Due in May 2021 (End of Spring semester).
  • Budget: None

People

  • Reporting to: Professor Cucalon and America's Best Coffee & Donuts
  • Approving work: Professor Cucalon and America's Best Coffee & Donuts
  • Informed of progress: All group members twice a week and a weekly basis with Professor Cucalon via email and MS Teams.
